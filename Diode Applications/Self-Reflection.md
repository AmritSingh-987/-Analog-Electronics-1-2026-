# Short Reflection: Diode Applications

### What I Learned
Through this assignment, I learned how to practically apply different diode models to circuit analysis. I gained a clearer understanding of how to calculate thermal voltage and use the diode current equation. Additionally, I learned how to determine the behavior of a circuit by comparing the approximate model (accounting for the 0.7V built-in drop of a silicon diode) with the ideal model, and I saw mathematically why the ideal model is often a good approximation when the source voltage is much larger than the diode drop.

### Main Difficulty
The main difficulty I encountered was analyzing the multi-loop circuits and parallel diode configurations (such as circuits containing both Silicon and GaAs diodes). It required careful application of Kirchhoff's Voltage Law to determine which diodes turn on first and how they clamp the voltage for the rest of the parallel branches. Keeping track of the voltage drops and current splits required careful step-by-step calculation.
